### 1\. Investigating Online Businesses and Scams

The internet is full of fake tools, shady businesses, and scams. Many of them look completely real. This makes it very hard for normal users to verify what is safe and what is not. So, instead of manually checking multiple sources, you can use AI to:

*   Scan websites
*   Analyze patterns
*   Cross-check data
*   Identify hidden connections

**What AI can Investigate?**

AI can help you answer questions like:

*   Who owns this website?
*   Is this business linked to other scams?
*   Are there complaints or fraud reports?

For example, researchers built systems that [discovered](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2509.12181) over **52,000 scam websites** by analyzing patterns in search queries and known fraud domains.

**Red Flags AI can spot:**

*   No clear company identity
*   Fake promises (“guaranteed profit”)
*   Missing legal pages
*   Suspicious payment methods (crypto-only, no refunds)

**Real-World Use Case**

Suppose you find a tool claiming: “Earn $500/day using AI automatically.” So, instead of trusting it, you can use AI to:

*   Investigate the domain history
*   Check related websites
*   Find user complaints
*   Analyze if similar scams exist

### 2\. AI for Custom Browser Rules

The internet is full of annoyances:

*   Popups
*   Ads
*   Fake content
*   Clickbait
*   Slow-loading elements

Most people accept this as normal, but AI and custom browser rules can completely change your browsing experience.

One of the most powerful tools for this is uBlock Origin, a popular open-source content blocker.

**What are custom rules?**

Custom rules are filters you write to:

*   Block specific elements
*   Hide unwanted sections
*   Remove distractions

AI can help you create these rules even if you don’t know coding.

**Example**

Let’s say you want to hide “AI-generated spam articles” from Google results, so you can use a rule like: This removes results containing certain keywords. Now AI can:

*   Generate custom filters for your needs
*   Identify patterns in annoying content
*   Suggest better blocking strategies

**Real Use Cases**

*   Remove YouTube distractions
*   Block fake news sources
*   Hide specific sections of websites
*   Speed up browsing by blocking heavy scripts

### 3\. AI for Hidden Information Discovery

There is a lot of information online that is technically “public” but practically hidden. Just think about:

*   Government grants
*   Hidden discounts
*   Repair cost breakdown tricks
*   Legal rights or entitlements

Most people never find these, and it’s not because they don’t exist but because they are buried in complex documents, vague language, or scattered sources. So, AI can act like a _smart investigator_ here.

**What AI does differently?**

Instead of just searching keywords, AI can:

*   Reads long documents fully
*   Connects information across sources
*   Simplifies complex legal or technical language
*   Identifies patterns humans miss

For example:

*   A repair quote might hide unnecessary charges. AI can compare it with standard pricing and highlight overcharging.
*   A government website may list benefits in confusing terms. AI can translate that into simple, actionable steps.

**Real-world Use Cases:**

*   **Consumers save money** by spotting inflated prices
*   **Citizens claim benefits** they didn’t know existed
*   **Freelancers and businesses** discover grants or tax advantages

### 4\. AI for Identifying Electronic Parts from Images

This is one of the most underrated use cases. Suppose you open a device and see:

*   A circuit board
*   Unknown chips
*   Burnt components

Earlier, you had to:

*   Search manually
*   Guess part names
*   Visit local markets

Now AI can identify parts instantly from images.

**How can you do it?**

You upload an image of:

*   PCB (Printed Circuit Board)
*   Capacitor/resistor
*   IC chip

AI analyzes:

*   Shape
*   Markings
*   Layout
*   Connections

Then it tells you:

*   Part name
*   Function
*   Specifications

Even experts struggle to identify parts without labels, but AI can:

*   Recognize partial markings
*   Suggest similar components
*   Explain what the part does

This is helpful if you want the details of an electronic part to check whether it’s original or not.

### 5\. AI as a Smart Hardware Troubleshooter

Most people panic when their AC stops cooling or their fridge starts making noise. Traditionally, you either call a technician or search Google with terms like “AC is not cooling why?” but with AI, you can describe the exact symptoms in simple language:

*   My AC is running but not cooling
*   The fridge is making a clicking sound every 10 seconds
*   There is ice forming in the back panel

You can also upload images of:

*   Internal components
*   Wiring
*   Circuit boards
*   Error codes

AI combines both text and visual inputs to give a much more accurate diagnosis.

**Why does this work better than Google?**

Search engines rely on keywords, but AI understands the context.

For example:

*   It can connect “clicking noise + compressor delay” to a relay issue
*   It can analyze a burnt component from an image
*   It can guide step-by-step checks

This is similar to how AI systems analyze complex systems by correlating logs, components, and failures to find root causes quickly.

**What AI can actually help you do**

*   Identify the exact faulty part
*   Suggest whether repair or replacement is better
*   Provide step-by-step testing instructions
*   Estimate the cost before calling a technician

**Real-world workflow**

1.  You describe the issue
2.  Upload an image of the component
3.  AI asks follow-up questions
4.  AI gives probable causes ranked by likelihood
